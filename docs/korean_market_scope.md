# Korean Market Scope

This repository targets the Korean AI hiring market.

The project is designed to discover, review, and eventually collect AI-related job descriptions connected to Korean companies and Korea-relevant hiring organizations. It is not a foreign-market collection project, and it should not be reinterpreted as a broad global job market crawler.

## Target Entities

Target organizations include:

- Korean companies hiring AI, data, ML, LLM, research, or analytics talent
- Korea-based subsidiaries or teams of global organizations when the role is Korea-relevant
- Korean public, academic, or research organizations with AI hiring signals
- Korean startups, scaleups, large enterprises, and traditional companies with AI transformation signals

The target is not "AI companies only." The target is organizations likely to hire AI talent in Korea.

## Target Sources

Target sources may include:

- Korean job posting websites
- Korean company official career pages
- Korea-relevant ATS-backed hiring endpoints
- official APIs where available and approved
- public ATS/API endpoints where allowed
- JSON/RSS feeds where allowed
- sitemap-discoverable public job pages where allowed

## ATS Scope

Global ATS names such as Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby, Workday, SmartRecruiters, and SAP SuccessFactors are treated as infrastructure patterns. They may appear because Korean or Korea-relevant companies can use global ATS infrastructure.

Their presence does not mean this project is collecting foreign companies or foreign job markets.

## Non-Goals

This project does not aim to:

- crawl foreign job markets broadly
- collect foreign company JDs unless the hiring context is Korea-relevant
- bypass robots.txt, Terms of Service, login walls, CAPTCHA, anti-bot systems, or access controls
- use hidden APIs or unapproved endpoints
- start JD collection before source approval exists

## Scope Rule

When there is ambiguity, the reviewer should ask:

1. Is the hiring organization Korean or Korea-relevant?
2. Is the job source relevant to the Korean hiring market?
3. Is there traceable evidence for the scope decision?
4. Is collection allowed by policy and technical checks?

If the answer is unclear, the source or company should remain in review rather than being promoted.
